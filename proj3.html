No One Left Behind

Veteran Interview Archive, Instagram-First Storytelling, and Offline Pop-up Exhibition

Date

May 2023 – Jun 2025

Role

Content Strategy & Social Media Management

Tools

Instagram (Stories/Reels), Content Planning, Template Design, Community Management, On-site Exhibition Operations

Category

Nonprofit Campaign, Digital Content, Community Engagement, Experiential Activation

Overview

I worked on “No One Left Behind” with T&C Foundation, ‘translating veteran interview archives into Instagram-first digital content and running the channel to reach Gen Z (10s–20s)’. I continuously refined message structure and formats based on performance and audience feedback, then extended the project into a two-day ‘offline pop-up exhibition’ in Seongsu so online interest could become an on-site experience.

Challenge

The first challenge was that ‘the topic carried heavy context’, yet the story was not reaching the 10s–20s audience effectively on social platforms. A second challenge was operational: limited resources made production slower, posting cadence inconsistent, and follower drop-off more likely. Finally, when expanding into an offline exhibition, we needed to ‘preserve the project’s core message’ while designing an experience that moves visitors from viewing to participation.

Strategy

1) Always-on publishing system

To reduce drop-off from irregular posting, I prioritized an “always-on” system built around repeatable formats rather than one-off production. I ‘set a practical cadence and designed a workflow’ that could sustain planning, publishing, and community interaction with limited capacity.

2) Concept-led packaging for Gen Z

Instead of assuming the story would carry itself, I treated ‘how we deliver it’ as the core strategy. I packaged interview-based narratives into concept-driven formats with ‘a clearer hook and a lower entry barrier’, using Gen Z-friendly language and platform-native framing to make the content easier to start and easier to share.

3) Story-template efficiency

I built Instagram Story templates to speed up production while keeping tone and structure consistent. This allowed us to deliver key messages in a familiar, repeatable sequence and maintain visual continuity across posts.

4) Community-first operation

I designed the channel as a relationship loop, not a one-way feed. I actively managed touchpoints such as story reactions and comments, reflected audience requests where appropriate, and treated interaction as a retention asset to keep people returning.

5) Performance-based iteration loop

I broke performance down beyond views—checking which components improved attention and response—then reapplied what worked into the next content cycle. This ‘small improvements, repeated’ approach helped stabilize output and increase reach over time.

6) Online-to-offline experience extension

I treated accumulated digital assets as modular story units that could be reconfigured into an offline exhibition. The goal was to keep message consistency while introducing participatory elements so visitors could engage emotionally and leave with a personal takeaway.

Process

01

Research

Reviewed early content performance patterns and identified which formats and message structures ‘generated stronger engagement from the 10s–20s audience’. In parallel, I considered offline constraints and mapped how digital stories could translate into a physical viewing flow.

02

Insight

Concluded that reach would improve through ‘concept design and a clear hook’, not through longer explanations. Operationally, consistent posting required templates and a repeatable communication routine.

03

Promotion & Creative

Planned and produced Instagram Story-led content, built templates, and edited interview narratives into lower-friction formats. I also supported continuity content beyond interviews to keep the channel active and maintain conversation.

04

Execution

Operated the channel with consistent publishing and community interaction, then supported the pop-up exhibition by adapting digital content into an offline experience designed for understanding, empathy, and participation.

Execution

  • Planned and produced ‘Instagram-first content based on veteran interviews’, focusing on concept clarity and Gen Z-friendly delivery.
  • Built Story templates to improve production speed and maintain consistent tone and messaging.
  • ‘Managed the Instagram channel’ and audience communication to reduce drop-off and sustain engagement.
  • Supported the ‘offline pop-up exhibition’ by restructuring digital assets into a physical experience with participatory elements.
