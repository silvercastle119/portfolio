City Hall Partnership Marketing Project

Integrated Local Campaign, Content-to-Conversion Journey, and Offline Pop-up Activation

Date

Aug – Dec 2025

Role

Marketing Intern (Marketer & Brand Manager)

Tools

Instagram for Business, Content Production (Reels/Carousel), Front-end Development, Python, Event Operations

Category

Partnership Marketing, Local/District Marketing, Experiential Activation, Conversion

Overview

I interned as a marketer and brand manager at a branding and marketing agency and led a local business marketing project in partnership with a city hall. The goal was to drive district-level foot traffic and translate that traffic into sales growth and stronger brand awareness through an integrated campaign. I connected Instagram content, a campaign landing page, and offline pop-up events into one coherent customer journey so online interest could convert into real visits and participation.

Challenge

Store marketing was fragmented, with each business promoting independently, which diluted the district’s message and made it difficult for awareness to translate into real visits. The project also had to satisfy multiple needs at once—sales growth, street activation, and brand awareness—so relying on a single channel or one-off event would not be enough. As a result, the biggest gap was not “lack of content,” but a weak path from exposure to attendance and participation.

Strategy

Conversion-first journey design

I treated the campaign as a conversion system by clarifying the reason to visit for the 20s–40s audience, centralizing key information, and reducing participation friction across touchpoints.

Audience-led campaign planning

I designed an audience-led campaign flow for 20s–40s targets, aligning message direction, content formats, and offline participation to drive measurable foot traffic.

Process

01

Research

Conducted stakeholder and field research by interviewing 70+ store owners to identify needs around sales growth, street activation, and brand awareness, then mapped the 20s–40s audience context.

02

Insight

Diagnosed the core gap as “awareness not converting into visits,” and defined the key unlock as a conversion-first journey that clarifies the reason to visit and reduces friction from exposure to participation.

03

Promotion & Creative

Designed an audience-led campaign flow for 20s–40s targets and produced Instagram Reels/carousel content using Instagram for Business, aligning messaging and creative across touchpoints to drive organic reach (97K+ views).

04

Activation & Conversion

Planned and executed offline pop-up events (including a book-festival-style activation with 1,000+ visitors) and front-end developed a campaign landing page to centralize information, guide participation, and refine execution using social performance and foot-traffic signals.

Execution

Field research & planning

I grounded the campaign in field insights by speaking with owners from 70+ stores and synthesizing needs related to sales, street activation, and awareness into actionable priorities. I then designed an audience-led campaign flow for 20s–40s targets, translating district strengths into a clear message direction and participation plan.

Content execution & performance

I produced Instagram Reels and carousel posts using Instagram for Business and applied character assets to keep the tone consistent and make key messages instantly understandable, achieving 97K+ organic views.

Offline activation

To drive real-world visits, I planned and executed offline pop-up events including a book-festival-style activation that attracted 1,000+ visitors.

Conversion touchpoint (Landing page)

To support conversion, I planned and front-end developed a campaign landing page to centralize essential information and guide users toward participation. Throughout the project, I reviewed Instagram performance and foot-traffic data together and refined both online content and offline operations based on what was driving real visits.
