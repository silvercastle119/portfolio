Independent Bookstore Event

Experience-Led Campus Activation, End-to-End Promotion, and On-site Operations

Date

Jan – Apr 2025

Role

Planning & Operations

Tools

Desk Research, Communication & Coordination, Content/Asset Production, Run-of-Show Planning

Category

Campus Marketing, Experiential Activation, Community Growth

Overview

I planned and executed an on-campus offline reading event for the independent bookstore “Jisikeul Damda,” titled Independent Bookstore Event, to rebuild an active connection between the bookstore and university students.

Instead of relying on one-way announcements, I designed an experience-led activation where students could participate on-site and then continue engaging through owned channels after the event.

Challenge

The challenge was to convert 'interest in reading' into a concrete reason to visit—by packaging the bookstore as something students could experience together, not just a place to buy books.

Strategy

Experience-led reframing

I reframed the problem from static campus promotion to two-way interaction, designing the activation so participation would feel natural and drive real attendance.

Frictionless, shareable flow

I built a clear, easy-to-grasp event concept that reduces friction and increases shareability, then connected the on-campus experience to Instagram and an open chat to extend engagement beyond the event day.

Process

01

Research

Observed student culture and reading behaviors and reviewed campus promotion norms to define what would realistically motivate participation.

02

Insight

Defined two success conditions: two-way interaction that makes participation feel natural, and a simple concept that reduces friction and increases shareability.

03

Promotion & Creative

Planned the promotional flow and produced core assets end-to-end (concept direction → copy/visual design → production) to keep messaging consistent across touchpoints.

04

On-site Operations

Built a staffing and role plan for the event day (8 staff members), assigned shift-based responsibilities, and documented task ownership to reduce operational risk.

Execution

  • coordination: Worked with the school side to align on purpose, secure cooperation, and coordinate logistics required for an on-campus program.
  • Planned the promotional flow and produced core assets end-to-end to ensure consistency from concept to production.
  • Pre-event operations management: Managed the promotion window (25.03.21–25.04.09) through nine coordination touchpoints, tracking requests, updates, and deadlines to keep execution on schedule.
  • Built a staffing and role plan for the event day and supported day-of operations and issue handling to keep the experience running smoothly.
  • Retention path: Connected the on-campus experience to Instagram and an open chat to maintain post-event continuity.
